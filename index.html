This season

A table under the lemon tree

We open at seven and close when the last guest wants to leave. There is no rush here — only the sound of the courtyard, a carafe of wine from Nemea, and food that takes all day to make. The garden has been in the family since before we had a sign on the door.

Slow-cooked lamb shoulder
Local wine from Nemea

Three generations in one kitchen

Yiayia Eleftheria's pastitsio still opens the week. Her daughter changed nothing. Her granddaughter, Zoe, added one dish: a pasta made by hand each afternoon from flour, egg, and whatever the market had. That is the whole of our philosophy.

Read the menu

Open evenings, always

We are open Tuesday through Sunday, from 7pm until the last person finishes their dessert wine. We do not take reservations more than one week in advance — the garden has only twelve tables, and we like it that way.

What we cook

Everything changes with the season, with the market, with what the fishermen brought in. These are the dishes that are here right now. Prices include bread and olives to start.

Act I

Να αρχίσουμε

To Begin

Tzatziki της μαμάς

Our grandmother's tzatziki: strained yogurt, garlic that has been resting in olive oil all day, cucumber pressed dry, and dill picked from the pot by the door. Nothing from a jar.

5 €

Ταραμοσαλάτα

Cured grey mullet roe beaten with yesterday's bread, lemon, and oil until it holds. Pale pink, dense, a little salty — the kind that makes you ask for more bread.

6 €

Φέτα στο φούρνο

A whole block of feta, still in its paper, baked with cherry tomatoes and green peppers and a small fire of dried oregano. Brought to the table in the tin it cooked in.

Serves two
9 €

Σαλάτα χωριάτικη

Tomatoes from the Argolid plain, Kalamata olives, white onion, green pepper, capers, and a slab of feta. Dressed only with oil and a little salt. Nothing more is needed.

8 €

Κεφτεδάκια

Pork meatballs with spearmint, onion, and a little allspice — the old recipe, not the taverna shortcut. Pan-fried in olive oil, served warm with a wedge of lemon.

8 €
The cellar

Wine from the Nemea valley, ten kilometres away

We pour from three producers — all within a short drive. The Agiorgitiko grape of Nemea is deep, soft, and smells faintly of dried figs. Ask for the carafe. It is always the right decision.

Act II

Το κυρίως

The Heart of It

Αρνί κλέφτικο

Lamb shoulder sealed in parchment with rosemary, garlic, and lemon peel, slow-cooked from noon until evening. It falls from the bone. We make six portions a night and that is all there is.

Slow-cooked · Limited
19 €

Χειροποίητα ζυμαρικά

Zoe's pasta, rolled by hand each afternoon, cut wide and rough, with a sauce of sautéed wild greens, a little butter, and aged Graviera grated over the top at the table.

Handmade daily
16 €

Μπακαλιάρος

Salt cod soaked for two days, then fried in a light batter until the outside shatters. Served with skordalia — a thick paste of potato and garlic that is more honest than polite.

17 €

Παστίτσιο της γιαγιάς

The original recipe: thick tubular pasta layered with spiced ground beef, a béchamel that sets firm enough to cut with a knife, and thirty minutes more in the oven than is strictly necessary.

Tuesday & Wednesday only
14 €

Ψητό κοτόπουλο

Half a village chicken, marinated overnight in lemon, garlic, and dried thyme, then roasted over charcoal. The skin is the point. Served with roasted potatoes that cooked in the same pan.

15 €

Μουσακάς

Layers of aubergine, potato, and seasoned ground lamb, with a top of béchamel gone gold. It needs rest after baking — ours rests for an hour before we serve it. That patience is the whole recipe.

15 €
Act III

Τα γλυκά

The Sweet End

Κρέμα λεμόνι

A set cream of lemon juice, eggs, and good butter, made each morning and chilled until it trembles. We serve it in a ceramic bowl with a spoon of thyme honey on the side.

6 €

Γαλακτομπούρεκο

Crisp filo around a semolina cream set with vanilla and lemon, soaked after baking in a thin syrup of orange blossom water. The pastry stays crisp for exactly twenty minutes — we time it.

5 €

Φρούτα του κήπου

Seasonal fruit from the garden and the market: in summer, peaches in wine syrup; in autumn, figs with walnuts and honey; in winter, oranges with cinnamon. Ask what is today's.

5 €

Λουκουμάδες

Small fried dough balls, made to order, served in a heap with a drizzle of dark Cretan honey and a dusting of cinnamon. The kind of thing you order thinking you will share them.

5 €

Γλυκό κουταλιού

A spoon sweet from the family's preserve jars: sour cherry in summer, walnut in winter, quince in spring. Served with cold water and a small glass of mastiha liqueur. This one is on us.

To reserve a table Γαμω τη Παναγία

We have twelve tables in the garden. In summer they fill quickly — especially on Thursday through Saturday. Call us before six in the evening and we will hold a table for you. We do not take reservations through the internet, and we do not have a waiting list. If there is no space, we will tell you honestly and suggest tomorrow.

Getting here

Ten minutes
from the waterfront

From Syntagma Square, walk up Staikopoulou Street toward the Palamidi fortress. We are on your left, past the bookshop, in the courtyard behind the low iron gate. There is no sign on the street — only a small painted tile with our name. Parking is on Bouboulinas Street along the sea.

The garden has been here longer than the sign. It will be here after we are gone.

— Eleftheria Papadimitriou, founder, 1987
